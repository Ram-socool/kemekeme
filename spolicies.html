San Roque National High School - General Policies

The following guiding principles shall be observed by every student, parent/guardian, and member of the San Roque National High School community:

  • A. Admission, Retention, and Dismissal: Consistent with the guidelines of the Department of Education, San Roque National High School shall adhere to fair and transparent procedures regarding the admission, retention, and potential dismissal of students. Decisions related to academic performance, conduct, and attendance will be made in accordance with established school rules and regulations, ensuring due process.
  • B. In Loco Parentis: San Roque National High School recognizes its vital role in the holistic development of its students and will act in loco parentis (in place of the parent) during school hours and school-related activities. The school will exercise reasonable care and guidance concerning the students' welfare, safety, and adherence to school rules and DepEd policies.
  • C. Learning Resources: Students are encouraged to have the necessary learning resources to support their academic progress. While the school strives to provide accessible education, having the required textbooks and other materials is beneficial for effective learning. The school will endeavor to inform parents/guardians about required materials at the beginning of the school year.
  • D. Parent/Guardian Communication Regarding Grades: Parents/authorized guardians will be informed of their child's academic progress through established channels, such as the viewing of grades via a school portal (if available) or the distribution of report cards and certificates at the end of each quarter. The school encourages parents/guardians to actively monitor their child's performance.
  • E. School Visits by Parents/Guardians: To ensure a conducive learning environment and efficient communication, parents/authorized guardians who wish to physically visit the school are requested to observe the following:
    • e.1They are expected to wear proper and decent attire and adhere to all prevailing health and safety protocols implemented by the school and the local authorities.
    • e.2For discussions regarding a learner's academic performance or disciplinary concerns, parents/guardians are encouraged to schedule an appointment with the concerned teacher or school personnel through the adviser. This will allow for focused and productive communication.
  • F. Campus Safety and Security: San Roque National High School is committed to providing a safe and secure learning environment. All students, personnel, and visitors are expected to cooperate with campus security measures and report any concerns that may affect the safety and well-being of the school community. This includes adhering to school rules related to conduct and security protocols.

Honor Roll Policies

  • A. The school follows the Policy Guidelines on Awards and Recognitionor the K to 12 Basic Education Program (DepEd Order No. 36, 2016). To wit:
    • a.1 Classroom Awards are given to learners in each class or section. Recognition may be given per quarter, semester, or at the end of the school year. Awardees are given merit by the adviser and/or subject teachers for their outstanding performance in class. (e.g. Performance Awards for Preschool, Academic Excellence Awards, and Perfect Attendance)
    • a.2 Grade-level Awards are given to qualified learners at the end of the school year. The Awards Committee (AC) evaluates candidates based on criteria. (e.g. Academic Excellence Awards, Conduct Awards, Leadership Awards)
    • a.3 Special Recognition is awarded to learners who have represented the school in competitions at any level—district, division, regional, national, or international.
  • B. Academic Excellence Award as Classroom Award: This is given quarterly to learners in grades 1–12 who achieve an average of at least 90 and pass all subjects. Grades are reported as whole numbers based on DepEd Order No. 8, s. 2015.
